Where it started
The Story

Twelve years of showing up, one Marine Street studio at a time.

Jen started teaching online in 2011, and in 2014 she opened the doors at 1212 Marine Street. The name "Jenstar" is hers, the studio is hers, and a dozen years in she still owns it, runs it, and teaches on the schedule.

That kind of continuity means something. Most boutique fitness in Green Bay is either a chain franchise with a rotating manager or a small studio that runs one modality. Jenstar runs four: yoga in six distinct styles, barre sculpt with light weights, strength training, and an adult dance program that covers multiple genres.

"Movement should fit where you are right now. First class or Tuesday morning before work, there is a spot for you."
Jen was featured on The Green Bay Health Project Podcast, Episode 23, talking about movement, community, and what it takes to build a studio in Green Bay. A good listen if you want to hear the story in her own words.

Common Questions

Good questions before your first class.

Do I need experience to join a class? +
Not at all. Most classes at Jenstar are designed to be accessible to any experience level. The beginner yoga classes and restorative sessions are great starting points if you are brand new. Just let Jen know it is your first time and she will point you to the right spot on the schedule.
How does the first-class-free offer work? +
Head to jenstaryoga.com, pick any class that fits your schedule, and book using the new-student offer. Your first class is on Jenstar. No credit card required to claim it, just show up.
What should I bring to my first class? +
Comfortable workout clothes and a water bottle are the basics. The studio has mats and props available if you do not have your own. For barre and dance classes, grippy socks are recommended. Arrive five minutes early so Jen can say hello and show you around.
Where exactly is the studio located? +
Jenstar Movement Studio is at 1212 Marine Street, Suite G, Green Bay, WI 54301. There is parking available nearby. If you have any trouble finding it, the best way to reach the studio is through the contact details on jenstaryoga.com.
Can I try more than one type of class? +
Yes, and that is exactly the point. Having yoga, barre, strength, and dance under one roof means you can build a full week of varied movement without needing a membership at multiple studios. Many Jenstar students mix two or three modalities in a typical week.
